Ceylon Tea Museum

The Ceylon Tea Museum is housed in a structure that was previously the Hatane Tea Factory, built in 1925, and is located about four kilometers south of Kandy. Before Clifford Ratwatte, the Chairman of the Sri Lanka Tea Board, merged the Ceylon Tea Museum inside the tea industry in 1998, it had been abandoned for more than ten years. The building was renovated for its new purpose as a museum, and on December 1st, 2001, it was formally opened to the public with the help of the Tea Board and the Planters' Association of Sri Lanka.
